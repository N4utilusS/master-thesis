	\section{Swarm Engineering}
	
	This section is largely inspired by~\cite{brambilla2013swarm}, a reviewing article on swarm engineering. For \c{S}ahin, swarm robotics is defined as \quot{the study of how large numbers of relatively simple physically embodied agents can be designed such that a desired collective behavior emerges from the local interactions among agents and between the agents and the environment}~\cite{csahin2005swarm}. Swarm robotics can be separated from other robotic studies by the following characteristics (\cite{brambilla2013swarm}):
	
\begin{itemize}
\item Robots are \emph{autonomous}
\item Robots evolve \emph{in the environment} and can interact with it
\item Robots' interactions are \emph{local} (sensors and communications)
\item No \emph{centralised control} or \emph{global knowledge}
\item Robots \emph{cooperate} to achieve a certain goal
\end{itemize}
